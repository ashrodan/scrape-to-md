Let's chat about the nbn™ boxes—those nifty devices that connect your home to the internet. Depending on your connection type, you'll have either a Network Termination Device (NTD) or a Network Connection Device (NCD). Here's a simple breakdown:

Fibre to the Premises (FTTP):

With FTTP, a fibre optic cable runs directly to your home. Inside, you'll find an NTD, which is the main hub connecting your home to the nbn™ network. Your modem/router plugs into this box to get you online.

An FTTP NTD is a white/cream box that is attached to the wall at your property. It is usually located in the garage, master bedroom, master bedroom wardrobe or the living room. If you are in a apartment building and you are unable to locate your NTD please ask for assistance from your building management. Keep in mind that each property should have its own NTD onsite inside the property. 

If you are missing an NTD it may be that one is yet to be installed. In this case, when we organise your nbn service we will book an nbn appointment so a technician can come out to install one. 

 

Hybrid Fibre Coaxial (HFC):

HFC uses a combination of fibre optics and existing cable TV lines. In this setup, you'll also have an NTD inside your home. Connect your modem/router to this device to access the internet.

An HFC NTD is a black color box that is provided by the nbn to connect to the network. You will need this device if you are in an HFC serviceable area. Each box is location coded so you are unable to use an box that you brought from another property. 

In most cases when you move into your property there should be an NTD. However if the last tenant took it (that's naughty) then we will need to organise one to be sent out to you. If you are in a new build property, there may not be a NTD as you are the first tenant, in this case we will send one out or a technician appointment might be required. There are no costs associated with this. 

Once you have located your NTD, you will need to plug your modem or modem router into the UNI-D port using an ethernet cable to be connected to the nbn network. 

 

Fibre to the Curb (FTTC):

FTTC brings fibre to a pit near your curb, with the final stretch using existing copper lines. For this connection, you'll have an NCD inside your home. Your modem/router connects to the NCD to get you online.

Each of these devices plays a crucial role in delivering internet to your home. They act as the bridge between the nbn™ network and your modem/router, ensuring you stay connected.

An FTTC NCD is location coded, meaning that you cannot bring it from another property.
